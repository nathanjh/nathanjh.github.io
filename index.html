Hiking the Romero Canyon Trail

In the Santa Barbara Front-Country

Connecting Trails

  • Ocean View Trail

    This historic trail roughly follows the crest of the mountain range (with the Pacific Ocean and Channel Islands in sight), and connects with the Romero trail as it emerges from the canyon just before topping out at the saddle.
  • Blue Canyon Trail

    After passing over the saddle, the Romero trail continues on back down the other side of the mountain and into the back-country as the Blue Canyon trail--named for the large deposits of blue serpentine rock visible in the canyon walls.

Overview

Flirting with a long-defunt service road, this historic route from the Pacific Ocean to the Santa Ynez valley winds relatively quickly up to the Romero Saddle. On its way, passing through lush, shaded riparian and oak woodlands up into the sandstone-studded high chaparral along the crest of the Santa Ynez mountains.

Stats

Altitude Gain: 2,184 ft
Highest Point: 3,179 ft (water tower at saddle)
Distance to saddle (trail only): 3.6 mi
