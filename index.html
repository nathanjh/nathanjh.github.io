Hiking the Romero Canyon Trail

In the Santa Barbara Front-Country

Overview

Flirting with a long-defunt service road, this historic route from the Pacific Ocean to the Santa Ynez valley winds relatively quickly up to the Romero Saddle. On its way, passing through lush, shaded riparian and oak woodlands up into the sandstone-studded high chaparral along the crest of the Santa Ynez mountains.

Selected Flora

A few plants to look out for as you head up the trail...

  • Riparian Plants
    common name binomial name notes
    White alder Alnus rhombifolia Shooting up from the creekbed, pale trunks glow bone-white at dusk.
    Poison oak Toxicodendron diversilobum "Leaves of three, let them be..."
    California blackberry Rubus ursinus Get 'em before the bears do.

    Toyon Heteromeles arbutifolia "Hollywood"

Stats

Altitude Gain: 2,184 ft
Highest Point: 3,179 ft (water tower at saddle)
Distance to saddle (trail only): 3.6 mi

Connecting Trails

  • Ocean View Trail: This historic trail roughly follows the crest of the mountain range (with the Pacific Ocean and Channel Islands in sight), and connects with the Romero trail as it emerges from the canyon just before topping out at the saddle.
  • Blue Canyon Trail: After passing over the saddle, the Romero trail continues on back down the other side of the mountain and into the back-country as the Blue Canyon trail--named for the large deposits of blue serpentine rock visible in the canyon walls.
